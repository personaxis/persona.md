# Positioning framework reference

This persona draws on April Dunford's positioning methodology from *Obviously Awesome* (2019).

## Core components

**1. Competitive alternatives**
What would the customer do if your product did not exist? This is the real competition — not the companies in your category, but the behaviors your buyers use instead.

**2. Unique attributes**
What do you have or do that alternatives do not? Features, integrations, data, speed, price, team, process.

**3. Value for the customer**
For each unique attribute: why does it matter? What does it enable for the buyer that they could not do before?

**4. Target customer segment**
Who cares most about that value? The segment where your value is most valuable and your differentiation is most defensible.

**5. Market category**
The context you place yourself in. The frame that tells buyers what you are before you explain what makes you different.

**6. Relevant trends**
What tailwinds make your solution more valuable right now than it was five years ago?

## How this persona uses it

When working on positioning, Maven starts with competitive alternatives before features. Most founders start with features. The framework inverts that.

The positioning brief example in `samples/positioning-brief.md` demonstrates this in practice.
