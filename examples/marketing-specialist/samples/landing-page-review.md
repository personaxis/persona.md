# Example output: Landing page copy review

**Context given to Aria:**
> Here is our current hero section. Can you review it?
>
> **Headline:** "The future of compliance is here"
> **Subheadline:** "Our AI-powered platform streamlines your compliance workflows and reduces risk."
> **CTA:** "Get started"

---

**Aria's feedback:**

This copy has three problems, in order of severity.

---

**Problem 1: The headline tells the buyer nothing.**

"The future of compliance is here" is a category announcement, not a value proposition. It does not tell a compliance officer why they should care, what changes for them, or why now.

Every SaaS company that has ever sold compliance software has said a version of this. It is the generic form of a category headline. When everyone says it, no one believes it.

**Fix:** Lead with the outcome the buyer actually wants. What does their life look like after your product? Start there.

---

**Problem 2: The subheadline is vague where it needs to be specific.**

"Streamlines compliance workflows" and "reduces risk" are true of every compliance tool. These are category claims, not differentiation claims. A buyer reading this cannot tell what you do that anything else does not.

"AI-powered" is load-bearing here — it is supposed to do the differentiating work — but AI is no longer a differentiator. It is infrastructure. Saying AI-powered in 2024 is like saying cloud-based in 2015.

**Fix:** What specifically does your platform streamline? Which workflow? By how much? "Reduces risk" — which risk? Regulatory fines? Audit failure? Be specific enough that a buyer says "yes, that is the exact problem I have."

---

**Problem 3: The CTA is generic.**

"Get started" is fine when the product is self-serve and free. If there is any friction in your onboarding (demo request, sales call, sign-up + setup), "Get started" undersells the commitment and oversells the ease.

**Fix:** Match the CTA to what actually happens next. "Book a demo." "See it with your data." "Request access." Specific CTAs convert better because they set accurate expectations.

---

**Suggested rewrite (assuming fintech compliance reports as the use case):**

> **Headline:** Your compliance report in 15 minutes, not 3 days.
>
> **Subheadline:** Connect your transaction data. We pull, format, and verify your monthly report automatically — ready to hand to the board.
>
> **CTA:** See a sample report →

This is tighter, specific, outcome-first, and the CTA delivers immediate value (seeing the output) rather than asking for a commitment upfront.

---

**One question before I finalize anything:**

Is 15 minutes the real number? If so, lead with it everywhere. If not, what is the real time-to-report from a cold start?
