# Example output: Positioning brief

**Context given to Aria:**
> We built a tool that automatically generates compliance reports for fintech startups. It pulls from your transaction data and outputs a PDF. Our users are usually CTOs or compliance officers. Competitors are manual consultants and legacy software like ComplyAdvantage.

---

**Aria's output:**

Before I write anything, let me reflect back what I'm hearing — because the brief as stated has a positioning problem worth fixing first.

**What you described:** A tool that generates compliance reports automatically.

**The actual value:** Compliance officers at fintech startups can stop spending 3 days a month assembling a report by hand, hand it to the board, and move on.

Those are different products. The first is a report generator. The second is time back and risk reduction. Buyers pay for the second one.

---

**Recommended positioning:**

**ICP:** Compliance officers and CTOs at Series A–B fintech companies (20–150 employees) operating under PSD2, AML, or similar regulatory frameworks. Currently doing this manually or with a consultant who charges by the hour.

**Their pain:** Manual compliance reporting is a recurring time tax. Every month, someone spends days pulling data, formatting it, verifying it, and praying nothing changed in the regulatory landscape. It is high-stakes, low-leverage work.

**What you are:** The tool that turns that recurring tax into a 15-minute review.

**Differentiation from consultants:** Consultants give you a deliverable. You give them a capability. Consultants bill for time. You are a fixed cost that goes down per-report as the company scales.

**Differentiation from legacy software:** ComplyAdvantage is risk data infrastructure. You are output. They are not substitutes — you could position against the DIY workflow, not against them.

---

**Suggested headline options:**

1. `Compliance reports in 15 minutes. Not 3 days.`
2. `Automate your compliance reporting. Ship the thing you actually built.`
3. `Your fintech is growing. Your compliance reporting shouldn't be a bottleneck.`

Option 1 is the strongest. It is specific, falsifiable, and buyer-outcome-first.

---

**What I need from you to tighten this further:**

- What does the 15-minute figure actually look like? What does a user do vs. what does the tool do?
- Any customer quotes about what they used to spend their time on?
- Is there a specific regulatory framework (PSD2, SOC 2, AML) where you are strongest?
